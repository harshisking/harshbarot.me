Off The Books

Last Updated: 09/01/2026 • Status: On-Hold

The room was washed with blinding lights. Cigarette smoke lingered in the air.
One table. Three chairs.
One was occupied. The other two waited on the opposite side.
He was cuffed to the table with one hand. The other held a cigarette, steady, unhurried.
I entered the room with my partner Dhruv Siri. My name is Ram Mohan. We had a single file between us - just one - with the name Kian Dharm.

He had volunteered to give us information on Dante, the crime lord the state had decided not to take alive.
We took the vacant chairs. Dhruv had already begun to tap his pen, “There is nothing in his file”, he whispered. How could a man with no record nor any connections would know about Dante.
“Mr. Dharm, my officers tell me you have information about Dante. They also said that your demands are… uncommon.” I said, “They are” he spoke with utter calmness and politeness.

“Mr. Dharm, what information are you willing to give us?”, “his location” he replied. “And how” I asked carefully, “did you find him?”. “It's not hard to find anyone for me.” he replied.
“Well then pray tell”,
“You fulfil your end of the deal, then I would mine”,
“You want a free ticket out of the country with a new identity. What do you think assures us that your intel is valid”, I spoke pointing to his file, “when we don’t even know who you are”,
“My father worked with Dante... more like he worked for my father”,
“I think this is it, you will be charged for wasting the governments resources”,
“A week ago, ” he said, “Bhatt was found dead, shot himself, but did he… Kareem had killed him.”
“How did he know?” I asked myself. The crime scene was so clean. It took us 6 days to figure this out, let alone a common man but he was not a common man, was he?
